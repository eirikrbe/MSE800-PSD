# Southern Alps Explorer: A 7-Day South Island Adventure

*7 days | December | Christchurch to Queenstown | mid-range budget | by rental car*

This 7-day self-drive itinerary takes you on a stunning journey through New Zealand's South Island, starting in Christchurch and culminating in Queenstown. You'll experience breathtaking alpine landscapes, learn about Maori culture, and indulge in local food and wine, all while enjoying invigorating hikes in the summer sun. Designed for a mid-range budget, this trip balances scenic drives with ample time for exploration and relaxation.

> **Feasibility:** Feasible as requested. This itinerary provides a good balance of driving and activities within the specified constraints for a South Island adventure.

## Day 1 — Lake Tekapo

**Getting there:** Christchurch to Lake Tekapo via SH1 and SH79/SH8, approximately 3 hours 15 minutes.

### Church of the Good Shepherd (0.5h)
*Pioneer Drive, Lake Tekapo*

This iconic stone church offers stunning views over Lake Tekapo and the Southern Alps. It's a peaceful spot perfect for capturing incredible photos and reflecting on the natural beauty of the Mackenzie Basin.

### Tekapo Hot Springs (2.5h)
*64 Lakeside Drive, Lake Tekapo*

Relax and unwind in the thermal pools with incredible panoramic views of Lake Tekapo and the surrounding mountains. A perfect way to soothe muscles after your drive and enjoy the Mackenzie night sky.

## Day 2 — Aoraki / Mount Cook Village

**Getting there:** Lake Tekapo to Aoraki / Mount Cook Village via SH8 and SH80, approximately 1 hour 15 minutes.

### Hooker Valley Track (3.5h)
*Hooker Valley Road, Aoraki / Mount Cook National Park*

This popular and relatively easy walk offers spectacular views of Aoraki/Mount Cook, glaciers, and milky-blue glacial lakes. It's a well-maintained track with swing bridges, perfect for a refreshing summer hike.

### Sir Edmund Hillary Alpine Centre (1.5h)
*The Hermitage Hotel, Aoraki / Mount Cook Village*

Explore the history of mountaineering in the region and learn about Sir Edmund Hillary's achievements. The 3D theatre offers an immersive experience of the national park's stunning landscapes.

## Day 3 — Wanaka

**Getting there:** Aoraki / Mount Cook Village to Wanaka via SH80, SH8, and SH6, approximately 2 hours 45 minutes.

### Wanaka Tree (That Wanaka Tree) (0.5h)
*Ardmore Street, Wanaka*

This iconic willow tree growing out of Lake Wanaka is a popular photography spot. It's a beautiful, tranquil place to visit, especially in the late afternoon light, representing the serene beauty of the town.

### Roys Peak Track (lower section or viewpoint) (2h)
*Wanaka-Mount Aspiring Road, Wanaka*

While the full track is long, a shorter walk along the lower section or simply enjoying the views from the carpark area offers stunning panoramic vistas of Lake Wanaka and the surrounding mountains, perfect for a summer afternoon.

## Day 4 — Wanaka

**Getting there:** No driving today.

### Rippon Vineyard & Winery (2h)
*246 Mount Aspiring Road, Wanaka*

Enjoy a wine tasting at one of New Zealand's most picturesque vineyards, overlooking Lake Wanaka. Learn about their biodynamic practices and sample their acclaimed Pinot Noir and Riesling, reflecting the region's excellent wine culture.

### Puzzling World (2h)
*188 Wanaka-Luggate Highway, Wanaka*

A quirky and fun attraction featuring illusion rooms and a giant maze. It's a light-hearted activity that offers a unique contrast to the natural landscapes and provides entertainment for all ages.

## Day 5 — Queenstown

**Getting there:** Wanaka to Queenstown via Crown Range Road (SH8C), approximately 1 hour 15 minutes. Note: This road is scenic but winding.

### Arrowtown Chinese Settlement (1.5h)
*Buckingham Street, Arrowtown*

Explore the preserved remnants of the Chinese gold miners' village from the 1860s. This historical site offers a fascinating glimpse into the challenging lives of early Chinese immigrants in New Zealand, providing cultural insight.

### Queenstown Gardens (1.5h)
*Park Street, Queenstown*

Take a leisurely stroll through these beautiful botanical gardens located on a peninsula jutting into Lake Wakatipu. It's a peaceful escape with disc golf, roses, and stunning lake views, perfect for unwinding after the drive.

## Day 6 — Queenstown

**Getting there:** No driving today.

### TSS Earnslaw Lake Cruise & Walter Peak High Country Farm (4h)
*Steamer Wharf, Beach Street, Queenstown*

Enjoy a scenic cruise across Lake Wakatipu on the historic TSS Earnslaw steamship to Walter Peak High Country Farm. Here you can watch a sheep shearing demonstration and enjoy a delicious BBQ lunch, combining heritage, scenery, and local food.

### Queenstown Gondola and Luge (2.5h)
*Brecon Street, Queenstown*

Take the Skyline Gondola up Bob's Peak for incredible panoramic views of Queenstown and Lake Wakatipu. Enjoy a thrilling luge ride down, a fun and active way to experience the stunning scenery.

## Day 7 — Queenstown

**Getting there:** No driving today.

### Ben Lomond Track (lower section) (3h)
*Accessed via Skyline Gondola, Queenstown*

For a final hike, take the gondola up and walk part of the Ben Lomond Track. Even the lower sections offer incredible views and a challenging but rewarding experience, perfect for a fit 30-year-old.

### Fergburger (1h)
*42 Shotover Street, Queenstown*

Indulge in a famous Fergburger, a Queenstown institution known for its delicious gourmet burgers. It's a casual and satisfying way to cap off your South Island adventure, experiencing a local food favourite.

## Packing tips

- Pack layers of clothing, including thermal base layers, a fleece, and a waterproof/windproof jacket, as weather in the mountains can change quickly even in December.
- Bring sturdy hiking boots or shoes suitable for varied terrain, as you'll be enjoying several walks and hikes.
- Don't forget sun protection: a wide-brimmed hat, sunglasses, and high-SPF sunscreen are essential due to New Zealand's strong UV rays in summer.
- Include swimwear for hot pools and lake activities, alongside a quick-drying towel.
- A reusable water bottle is a must for staying hydrated during hikes and reducing plastic waste.
