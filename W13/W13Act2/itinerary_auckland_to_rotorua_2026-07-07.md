# North Island Explorer: Culture, Craters & Coastlines

*7 days | December | auckland to rotorua | mid-range budget | by rental car*

This 7-day North Island adventure begins in Auckland, leading you through stunning natural landscapes, rich Maori culture, and delicious local flavours. Experience the magic of glowworm caves, the geothermal wonders of Rotorua, and the beautiful beaches of the Coromandel, all while enjoying scenic drives and invigorating hikes tailored for an active traveller. Your journey culminates in Rotorua, immersing you in its unique cultural and volcanic heart.

> **Feasibility:** Feasible as requested. This itinerary provides a good balance of driving and activities within the specified constraints, focusing on the North Island's highlights between Auckland and Rotorua.

## Day 1 — Auckland

**Getting there:** No driving today

### Auckland War Memorial Museum (3h)
*Auckland Domain, Parnell, Auckland*

Explore New Zealand's natural history, Maori cultural treasures, and war history. It's an excellent introduction to Aotearoa's heritage, perfect for understanding the local context before delving deeper into Maori culture.

### Parnell Village (2.5h)
*Parnell Road, Parnell, Auckland*

Wander through one of Auckland's oldest suburbs, known for its charming Victorian buildings, art galleries, boutiques, and cafes. Enjoy a leisurely afternoon exploring and finding a great spot for dinner.

## Day 2 — Waitomo Caves

**Getting there:** Auckland to Waitomo Caves via SH1 and SH39, about 2.5 hours

### Waitomo Glowworm Caves (1h)
*39 Waitomo Caves Road, Waitomo Caves*

Take a guided boat tour through the magical Waitomo Glowworm Caves, a truly unique natural spectacle. This iconic experience is a must-do and offers a fascinating insight into subterranean ecosystems.

### Ruakuri Cave (2h)
*Ruakuri Road, Waitomo Caves*

Embark on an accessible, wheelchair-friendly walking tour through Ruakuri Cave, offering a different perspective with impressive stalactites, stalagmites, and more glowworms. It's a great follow-up to the main glowworm cave.

## Day 3 — Rotorua

**Getting there:** Waitomo Caves to Rotorua via SH39 and SH3, then SH5, about 2 hours

### Te Puia (3h)
*Hemo Road, Tihiotonga, Rotorua*

Discover New Zealand's geothermal wonders and rich Maori culture at Te Puia. Witness the Pohutu Geyser erupt, explore mud pools, and observe traditional Maori carving and weaving schools in action, offering a deep cultural immersion.

### Redwoods Whakarewarewa Forest (2.5h)
*Long Mile Road, Whakarewarewa, Rotorua*

Enjoy a hike or walk amidst towering Californian Coastal Redwoods. There are various trails to suit different fitness levels, offering a serene natural escape and beautiful photo opportunities. A perfect way to get some exercise.

## Day 4 — Rotorua

**Getting there:** No driving today

### Wai-O-Tapu Thermal Wonderland (2.5h)
*201 Waiotapu Loop Road, Waiotapu*

Experience the vibrant colours and unique features of Wai-O-Tapu, including the Champagne Pool, Devil's Bath, and the Lady Knox Geyser (active at 10:30 AM daily). It's a stunning display of geothermal activity, easily reachable by a short drive or shuttle from Rotorua.

### Tamaki Maori Village Hangi & Cultural Experience (4h)
*1220 Hinemaru Street, Rotorua (pick-up point)*

Immerse yourself in an authentic Maori cultural experience, including a traditional Hangi meal cooked underground, powerful performances, and storytelling. This evening activity provides a memorable insight into Maori customs and hospitality.

## Day 5 — Tauranga

**Getting there:** Rotorua to Tauranga via SH30 and SH2, about 1 hour

### Mount Maunganui Summit Walk (2h)
*Pilot Bay Road, Mount Maunganui, Tauranga*

Hike to the summit of Mauao (Mount Maunganui) for panoramic views of the Bay of Plenty, Tauranga, and the Pacific Ocean. This popular track offers a rewarding challenge and stunning scenery, perfect for active travellers.

### The Strand Waterfront Dining (2h)
*The Strand, Tauranga*

Enjoy the vibrant atmosphere of The Strand waterfront, offering a variety of mid-range restaurants and bars. It's a great place to relax, enjoy local cuisine, and watch the boats in the harbour.

## Day 6 — Whangamata

**Getting there:** Tauranga to Whangamata via SH2 and SH25, about 1.5 hours

### Karangahake Gorge Historic Walkway (2.5h)
*State Highway 2, Karangahake*

Stop en route to Whangamata for a picturesque walk through the Karangahake Gorge. Explore old gold mining tunnels, swing bridges, and beautiful river scenery, offering a blend of history and nature. This is an excellent stretch-your-legs activity.

### Whangamata Beach (2h)
*The Esplanade, Whangamata*

Relax on the beautiful, long sandy beach of Whangamata, known for its surf and calm waters. It's perfect for a swim, a leisurely stroll, or simply soaking up the summer sun, offering a classic Kiwi beach experience.

## Day 7 — Rotorua

**Getting there:** Whangamata to Rotorua via SH25, SH2, SH30, about 2.5 hours

### Okere Falls Track (1.5h)
*Okere Falls Road, Okere Falls*

Enjoy a scenic walk along the Kaituna River, witnessing stunning waterfalls and watching rafters navigate the rapids. This easy track offers beautiful views and a chance to experience the natural beauty just outside Rotorua.

### Government Gardens and Rotorua Museum (exterior) (1.5h)
*Queens Drive, Government Gardens, Rotorua*

Stroll through the historic Government Gardens, admiring the beautiful architecture of the Rotorua Museum (currently closed for strengthening, but the exterior is magnificent) and the tranquil surroundings. It's a peaceful way to conclude your trip in Rotorua.

## Packing tips

- Lightweight, breathable clothing is essential for December's warm summer weather, along with swimwear for beaches and hot pools.
- Comfortable hiking shoes or sturdy walking sandals are a must for trails and town exploration.
- A waterproof jacket or light rain shell is always recommended, as New Zealand weather can change quickly, even in summer.
- Sun protection is crucial: bring a wide-brimmed hat, sunglasses, and high-SPF sunscreen due to the strong UV index.
- Insect repellent is advisable, especially for evenings and walks near water, to ward off sandflies and mosquitoes.
